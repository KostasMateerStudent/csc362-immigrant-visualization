Introduction

Our project aims to analyze the trends in nonimmigrant worker admissions to the United States over time. As an international student, Sathish has a personal interest in understanding the likelihood and trends of immigration to the US. On the other hand, Kostas' mother was an immigrant herself, giving him a unique perspective on the topic. Our project seeks to not only uncover the general trends but also investigate potential disparities within the US immigration system.

Questions

To gain deeper insights into the trends and disparities within the US immigration system, we will explore the following questions:

  • Which continents did the most nonimmigrant temporary workers come from in 2021 (workers and families)? Is it a matter of absolute population numbers or are some continents favored?
  • What are the disparities between the admitted nonimmigrants into the US? Are certain countries favored or is it a matter of absolute population numbers?
  • What was the effect of COVID-19 on the disparities between the admitted nonimmigrants into the US?
  • Where are these nonimmigrants entering the United States, and what factors contribute to their choice of destination?

Dataset

Our analysis is based on the 2021 U.S. Nonimmigrant Admissions Annual Flow Report, obtained from the US Department of Homeland Security. The dataset provides comprehensive information on nonimmigrant admissions to the United States, including detailed data gathered from Form I-94 arrival records on the number and characteristics of nonimmigrant admissions in Fiscal Year 2021. The dataset is vast and contains multiple tables, including information on the number of admissions over time, immigration status, and country of origin. Sure! In addition to the U.S. Nonimmigrant Admissions Annual Flow Report from the US Department of Homeland Security, we also used data from the World Bank to provide additional context for our analysis. Specifically, we obtained data on GDP per capita and population from the World Bank to explore potential correlations between a country's economic situation and the number of nonimmigrant temporary workers admitted to the US. By incorporating this additional data, we hoped to gain a deeper understanding of the factors that influence US immigration trends.
